A honeypot is a cybersecurity exploit designed to crawl and detect malicious 
activity that mimics vulnerable systems or services. 
Acting as a lure, the honeycomb appears to be a normal part of the network, 
attracting attackers to interact with it. 
There are two main types of honeycombs: low connectivity honeycombs, 
which do not reflect the actual processing patterns and simulate tasks, 
and highly connected honeycombs, which use real patterns to create more realistic
scenarios Honeypots detection of malicious behavior Analysis also, 
serves purposes such as acting as a deterrent, facilitating investigations,
and assisting in forensic investigations but the use of honey pots requires 
consideration of legal and ethical implications carefully, 
allowing the avoidance of potential legal consequences is important.
